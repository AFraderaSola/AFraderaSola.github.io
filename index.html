About

I am a PhD candidate in Quantitative Proteomics in the Institute of Molecular Biology. My research interests focus on bioinformatics, specially when it comes to omics data and its statistical analysis. My work revolves around analyzing NGS-based genomics and Quantitative Mass spectrometry-based proteomics data. I consider myself both collaborative and adaptable. I am always eager to tackle new and different projects. I am very enthusiastic to always share and develop my data visualization skills as a means towards better science communication. I am an open-minded person and enjoy having great relationships with my colleagues.

Projects

All Proteomics Genomics
RBP interactome characterization in yeast
Main PhD project - RNA binding proteins (RBPs) were selected according to their involvement in RNA pathways and immunoprecipitated. This allowed us to create a function-based network that enabled the assignment of new functionalities on novel and known RBPs.
Slides
Scoring function development for RNA-Seq differential expression assessment
Main M.Sc project - I developed a scoring function around three R-based RNA-Seq differential expression packages (DESeq2, edgeR, limma+voom). The main goal was to assess ow well packages perform and compare the their results in order to maximize (or restric, depenging on your criteria) the number of detected DEGs.
